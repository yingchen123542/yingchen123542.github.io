Research

Inclusive Voice Assistant

Reference: https://www.adweek.com/agencies/people-with-down-syndrome-frustrated-by-voice-devices-are-teaching-google-to-hear-them/

Analyzing speech patterns of people with Down Syndrome. Comparing the performaces of different algorithms used for voice recognition including Youtube transcript, IBM Watson transcript, Zoom transcript, and Microsoft Azure transcript through coding in Python.

Let's Read

Reference: https://in.pinterest.com/pin/386042999292960978/

Designing user interface and prototype for a reading app for hearing children of deaf adults. As the reading app reads the story out loud, words that are currently being read will be highlighted, so that parents can sign along, and children can learn both sign language and spoken language at the same time.

    Click here to see the paper

Air Quality Monitoring

Reference: presentation cover created by teammates Mimi Fhima, Krystal Flores, Tommy Rocha, and DJ Walia

Purple Air devices are set up in different buildings on Chapman University campus to monitor the level of humidity, temperature, and particulate matter(PM) including PM 1.0, PM 10, and PM 2.5. Air quality data is being collected evey hour, and analyzed to see which location has the worst air quality and the potential causes to air pollution.

    Click here to see the poster

Global Filter Study

Reference: https://www.jihosoft.com/photo/how-to-make-blurry-pictures-clear.html

Comparing the eye movements of children with Autism when they look at clear pictures and pictures with filters that make them blurry, in order to find the best way for children with Autism to stay focus and absorb information. Manually analyzing the videos of eye movements to see where and for how long children with Autism concentrated their gaze on certain spots in the provided pictures.
